About the Project

Building bridges through empathy

Delivered by IPSO gGmbH (International Psychosocial Organisation) in Friedrichshain-Kreuzberg, Berlin, the City.Zen project integrates professional psychosocial support with community-led programming. Trained community mobilizers — themselves from refugee and migrant backgrounds — design and deliver workshops, peer support groups, and creative activities under professional supervision.

Community Mobilizers

Individuals from refugee and migrant backgrounds trained to organise peer support groups, workshops, and community dialogues. In 2025, Arabic community volunteers delivered eight original projects reaching over 110 participants.

The mobiliser model — from identification, to training, to supervised delivery — is the structural heart of City.Zen.

Value Based Counselling (VBC)

IPSO's culturally sensitive, client-centred approach offers support in the client's native language. Since 2017, IPSO has delivered over 87,000 counselling sessions worldwide through its online platform.

VBC is a short-term intervention that restores psychological resilience, enabling individuals to navigate challenges and integrate successfully.

Creative & Cultural Formats

Puppet theatre, storytelling, film screenings, writing workshops, community cookbooks, and cultural outings serve as vehicles for psychosocial support — building connection and restoring agency.

Creative formats consistently attracted strong attendance, often from people who had not previously engaged with mental health services.

Three-Layer Approach

Professional psychosocial support, community mobiliser leadership, and structural outreach work together. Art and creativity are central — not peripheral — to trauma-sensitive community building.

Individual relationships create trust for group settings; group settings open doors to individual counselling. The two modes amplify each other.

Our Approach

A multimodal path to resilience

The project operates through three interlocking layers, with art and creativity as central vehicles for psychosocial support.

♡

Professional Psychosocial Support

Trained IPSO counsellors provide individual sessions, facilitate support groups, and supervise all community-led activities. All work is grounded in Value Based Counselling (VBC) and trauma-informed practice.

Weekly individual counselling sessions offered low-barrier, walk-in psychosocial support. Three parallel Arab women's support groups ran throughout the year, offering sustained therapeutic space to over 30 women.
+
☆

Community Mobiliser Leadership

Individuals from refugee and migrant backgrounds are trained and supported to design and deliver their own workshops and projects — ensuring cultural authenticity, peer trust, and sustainable community ownership.

In 2025, Arabic community volunteers delivered eight original workshops reaching 110+ participants. Twenty-one volunteers across all four communities completed structured training. The supervision process produced capable, reflective practitioners with skills in facilitation, group dynamics, and participatory design.
+
♫

Creative & Cultural Programming

Puppet theatre, storytelling, film screenings, writing workshops, and community outings serve therapeutic purposes: building connection, providing language for difficult experiences, and restoring agency.

A five-session film screening programme at Bona Cultural Centre brought together refugees, migrants, and Berlin residents. Three writing workshops used English as a shared neutral space across all language communities.
+
☍

Structural Outreach & Networking

Consistent presence at refugee shelters, partnerships with community venues and NGOs, and participation in Berlin-wide dialogues ensure the project is embedded in the communities it serves.

The team maintained partnerships with 15+ organisations including Bona Peiser, RuDi, Work-Out E.V., Refugio Berlin, Berlinische Galerie, and 6+ refugee shelters. A landmark networking event at Refugio Berlin drew 80 participants.
+
⚔

Psychoeducation & Life Skills

Structured workshop series address daily challenges of migration — from sleep disorders and stress management to discrimination, seasonal depression, and decision-making under uncertainty.

The Farsi/Dari life-skills series reached 8–13 participants per session covering loneliness, self-confidence, stress management, discrimination, and seasonal depression. Ukrainian workshops addressed media literacy, public speaking, and anxiety.

Project Objectives

Six goals for lasting change

Click any objective to see the concrete actions being taken to achieve it.

Objective 01

Establish a Supportive Network

Create meaningful connections among diverse groups — volunteers, new arrivals, and native community members.

Identify and recruit community volunteers; organise meet-and-greet events; create platforms for ongoing communication and resource sharing.
Objective 02

Organise Joint Events

Facilitate events that encourage dialogue and shared experiences focused on trauma-informed practices.

Plan storytelling nights, panel discussions, cultural exchanges, film screenings, and writing workshops.
Objective 03

Promote Trauma Awareness

Educate community members about the impact of trauma on mental health and well-being.

Host workshops led by mental health professionals covering trauma-informed care, coping strategies, and empathy.
Objective 04

Encourage Collaborative Projects

Create opportunities for interaction and relationship-building through shared creative work.

Initiate community art projects, local celebrations, and cultural showcases involving participants from various backgrounds.
Objective 05

Empower Immigrants and Citizens

Ensure immigrants feel valued and engaged, contributing to their sense of belonging.

Develop mentorship and supervision programmes; provide platforms for immigrants to share skills; encourage participation in decision-making.
Objective 06

Utilise Art for Expression

Leverage art to transcend language and cultural barriers, allowing individuals to express their experiences.

Organise creative workshops in visual arts, music, theatre, and writing, followed by facilitated discussions.

Looking Ahead

The 2026 roadmap

Building on 127 activities and 773+ participants in 2025, the project deepens community ownership, expands cross-community programming, and builds its evidence base.

Expand Film Screening Programme

Scale from 5 to 10+ screenings at Bona Cultural Centre — the format's power as a low-threshold, cross-community entry point is proven.
